academic_decline: Observable decrease in cognitive performance, memory retention, or attentional regulation, often influenced by environmental stressors, fatigue, or emotional dysregulation.

default_mode_disruption: Dysregulation of the brain‚Äôs default mode network, leading to disorganized internal thought, impaired memory encoding, and attentional dysfunction.

#### üêù Neuroimmune and Inflammatory Processes
microglial_activation: Transition of microglia from surveillant to reactive phenotype, often triggered by stress, injury, or pathogens; involves cytokine release and synaptic pruning.

neuroinflammation: An immune response within the CNS involving microglia, astrocytes, and inflammatory cytokines; contributes to neurodegeneration and cognitive decline.

oxidative_stress: Accumulation of reactive oxygen species (ROS) damaging lipids, proteins, and DNA in neurons; linked to aging, stress, and neurodegeneration.

disruption(pnn): Breakdown or remodeling of perineuronal nets, extracellular matrix structures that stabilize synapses and regulate plasticity; associated with memory instability and neural vulnerability.

synaptic_pruning: Activity-dependent or pathological removal of synapses; developmentally necessary but excessive in conditions like schizophrenia and Alzheimer's disease.

#### üß™ Neuroendocrine and Hormonal Dynamics
low(neurosteroid_levels): A reduction in levels of endogenous neuroactive steroids (e.g., estradiol, allopregnanolone), impairing GABAergic signaling, neuroplasticity, and stress regulation.

aromatase_suppression: Downregulation of the aromatase enzyme responsible for converting androgens into estrogens in the brain; impacts hippocampal estradiol availability and neuroprotection.

estradiol_decline: Decrease in brain-derived or circulating estradiol, contributing to mood instability, memory deficits, and neurodegeneration, particularly in menopausal individuals.

glucocorticoid_excess: Elevated levels of cortisol or corticosterone from chronic stress exposure; impairs hippocampal synapses, neurogenesis, and memory encoding.

bdnf_deficiency: A reduction in brain-derived neurotrophic factor (BDNF), critical for synaptic strengthening and memory formation, often caused by stress or genetic variation.

chronic_stress: Prolonged activation of the HPA axis and glucocorticoid exposure, leading to reduced neurogenesis, dendritic retraction, and impaired memory performance.

sleep_deprivation: Inadequate sleep duration or quality that impairs hippocampal consolidation, disrupts circadian regulation, and lowers glymphatic clearance of metabolic waste.

social_isolation: Environmental deprivation of social contact that contributes to neuroinflammation, depressive phenotypes, and reduced neurogenesis.

environmental_enrichment: Exposure to complex and stimulating environments that promote synaptic growth, neurogenesis, and improved cognitive flexibility.

cognitive_overload: Excessive mental demands that exceed working memory capacity, often leading to performance breakdown, attentional lapses, and anxiety.

#### üë¥ Aging and Degenerative States
